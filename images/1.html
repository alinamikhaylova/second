Values of the Product Illustration

How to show the value of the product and what are the product illustration values

Katarzyna Dziaduś
Follow
Feb 10, 2019 · 6 min read

I found myself in this field and it is certainly not the easy one. But I was super lucky because there was someone on that journey who showed me how significant the product illustration can be, how to think about it and how to solve problems with it. Thinking about it holistically in the approach to the product. Treating it as a huge support for the brand and using product illustration as a tool for understanding has become an incredible adventure for me to tell you about.

What is a product illustration?

Product illustration is an element of product humanization, which can be an object, a mobile application or a website. It can be a new technology or a completely new invention. And here, as an illustrator, you look at the problem, or in other words — at the message. What is new, what are the values of the item? How to show those values that will be crucial for the user? How to make the illustration understandable and instead of unnecessary chaos lead to an aha moment?

How to find the right values of the product to show.

Every product is a new challenge. Sometimes we get an excellent brief, which guides us through research and analysis and shows exactly how to present something to the user. Usually, we need to do more research on our own and ask customer support questions to find the key point for which the user will not only understand the product but also want to come back to it.

Good questions to ask about the product to find the right values:

  • What is the product?
  • What is the main value of the product?
  • Who is the target?
  • What is the vision and mission of the company?
  • What kind of language does the brand speak?
  • How do they want to be perceived?
  • What does the research say about it?
  • What as a user I would like to see in the product?
  • How does the competition do it?

So, how does the illustration deal with the answer to these questions?

Values of the product illustration:

Support the product and doesn’t shout for the attention

(Bad teacher)

The product illustration should be visually pleasing because if it is not, it will surely cause more confusion than benefits. However, being only eye candy without concrete support of the message is the only ornamentation. And if I like such ornaments myself, I still learn that even with illustrations, less is often more. The illustration that supports the product is primarily the one that solves the problem. It allows better understanding by users and translates in a calm and exerted way, without shouting.

Doesn’t distract but direct eyes on the most important parts

(Annoying child)

Hey, look here and here and there. Don’t miss it either. Marking every aspect of a product as something highly important is connected with building irritation rather than encouraging the user to understand what the product and its aspects are. P.S.: This world does not always have to be built by micro people. Someone has described them like a plague and I always enjoy the term, but I understand it more and more.

The product illustration is a part of the branding

(The book)

A well-designed and well-described illustration system is something that makes me get into procrastination. I could watch these beautiful grids, layouts, colors and uses for hours. Interestingly, in the case of illustration, such a precise definition of the system as to how to create and use it gives measurable benefits. HERE an excellent example from Shopify.

They created amazing bound between the user and the brand, great job Shopify!

Translates the content and educates

(Teach me, master)

You download the application from the store, you open it and the first thing you see is the onboarding screens. Colorful elements, characters show you how to use the functions. Super, now you will remember. And what if there is only raw text on these pages? We probably know the answer to this question, referring to the statement that the vast majority of us are opticians.
And what does it look like in a strictly educational application? Duolingo copes well with it. Each exercise is illustrated with a pleasant illustration. Here is an example of how it looks like. (Si, aprendo Español)

Humanizes the message

(Make new friends)

If you are creating a product and want to send a message, share information or just congratulate, then the illustration here can play a significant role. It is more likely that a pleasant picture will keep you for a moment and allow you to read what you would like to tell him.

An example? Revolut, the banking application, does it great, maybe I would read popups in this application without any illustrations (Hey! It’s about my money!) but thanks to them I feel completely safe there and I always enjoy some new illustrations from them, especially when we celebrate something together.

Visually attractive

(Eye-candy)

Certainly, the aesthetic value shouldn’t be the most important one, but if the illustrations are not pleasant and stylistically coherent, they will certainly cause confusion and misunderstanding. At the same time, this can make all the above aspects automatically excluded. When can only visually pleasing illustrations make sense? For example, where we have a lot of text and we want to overcome it somehow, such ornamentation is advisable!

Solves the problems

(Problem-solver)

To sum up the features of the product illustration, it is certainly a kind of solution to problems. This is a cure for misunderstanding and therapy for a better knowledge of our product. Sometimes a small visual change is enough to help you understand the product.

Every day I discover more and more common features when it comes to good product illustrations. The values I found are only a small part of what it has to say. I hope that this journey will never end because this constant exploration and problem-solving is the most interesting thing this field can offer. And I hope you’ll find something exciting in this also!
